Porcelain vs composite

Two beautiful ways to your new smile

We offer both porcelain and composite veneers, and we'll help you choose the right fit for your goals, timeline and budget.

Most popular

Porcelain veneers

Our most natural-looking option — highly translucent, stain-resistant and durable, designed to reflect light just like real enamel for a flawless, long-lasting finish.

  • Most natural, light-reflecting finish
  • Stain-resistant & hard-wearing
  • Typically completed over two visits
Single visit

Composite veneers

A more affordable option, often completed in a single visit with little to no enamel removal. Beautifully natural, though slightly less translucent than porcelain.

  • Single-visit option
  • More affordable upfront
  • Minimal to no preparation
Not sure which is right for you?

At your consultation we'll walk you through both, show you a digital preview of your new smile, and recommend the option that best suits your goals — for tooth colour, shape, size and your overall smile line.

The Horizon Dental veneer journey

A collaborative smile-design process

Your veneer treatment is more than a cosmetic procedure — it's a smile designed with you, step by step. Here's exactly what to expect.

≈ 45 minutes

1. Consultation & Digital Smile Design

We begin with a comprehensive assessment — high-resolution photos and a digital scan of your teeth. Using advanced software, we simulate your potential results and discuss your goals, preferences and lifestyle.

  • Smile analysis
  • Veneer types & benefits
  • Budget & timeline planning
≈ 1.5–2 hours

2. Temporary Mock-Up (“Test Drive”)

Once your design is finalised, we create a temporary mock-up so you can “test drive” your new smile — seeing and feeling what your new teeth will look like, with the chance to make changes before committing. You'll feel 100% confident before moving forward.

≈ 1–2 hours

3. Final Veneer Placement

On the day of placement, we carefully prepare your teeth (if needed) and bond your final veneers in place with precision. We check the fit, bite and aesthetics before finalising everything — the time depends on how many veneers are being fitted.

Good to know

Veneer questions, answered

Porcelain veneers are considered a long-term solution and often last many years. Because a small amount of enamel is removed during the process, they aren't reversible. Composite veneers are less invasive and can be removed or replaced more easily. We'll explain the trade-offs clearly at your consultation.

Absolutely. Your veneers are custom-designed to match the natural shape, colour and translucency of your teeth for a lifelike result. Porcelain in particular reflects light in a way that closely resembles natural enamel.

It depends on your goals. Some people choose 2–4 veneers to correct specific teeth, while others opt for 6–10 (or more) for a complete smile makeover. We'll help you determine the ideal number at your consultation.

No special products are required — just brush, floss and attend regular check-ups. If you grind or clench your teeth, we may recommend a nightguard to protect your veneers and help them last.

Porcelain veneers are stain-resistant but don't respond to whitening treatments. If you'd like a brighter smile, we recommend whitening your natural teeth beforehand so we can match the final veneer shade.
